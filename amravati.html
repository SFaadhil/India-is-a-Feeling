Discover Amravati

Amravati is one of Maharashtra's most vibrant university cities, located in the culturally rich Vidarbha region. Far from the bustle of metro India, this city offers exchange participants something truly rare — a deeply genuine and unfiltered Indian experience, rooted in community, simplicity, and intellectual life.

"Alladisthan" — the feeling of perfect contentment in the present moment — captures what exchange participants discover here. AIESEC in Amravati blends academic engagement with cultural warmth, creating exchanges that leave a lasting impact on every participant who visits.

City Highlights

What makes Amravati unforgettable.

Ambadevi Temple

Ambadevi Temple — one of Maharashtra's 51 Shakti Peethas — has been Amravati's spiritual centre for centuries, drawing pilgrims from across Vidarbha for the major festivals. The temple's ritual calendar shapes the city's identity more than any other institution. Visiting during Navratri gives an unmediated window into living religious practice at its most genuine.

Vidarbha Heritage

Vidarbha maintains a Marathi cultural identity shaped by the cotton economy, proximity to tribal communities, and distance from the Pune mainstream. The region's folk traditions — Gondi art, Warli painting, Povada ballads — are entirely distinct from what most visitors expect of Maharashtra. Exchange participants here encounter an India that goes almost entirely unvisited by outsiders.

Melghat Tiger Reserve

Melghat was one of India's nine original Project Tiger reserves, protecting 2,768 square kilometres of Satpura range forest north of Amravati. The park shelters tigers, leopards, wild dogs, and over 400 bird species — and receives far fewer visitors than more famous parks. The dense canopy, river gorges, and tribal Korku villages make it one of central India's most genuine wildlife destinations.

Sporting Culture

Sport in Amravati functions as social infrastructure — the Tapadia Stadium draws consistent crowds, and the city has produced nationally recognised athletes across multiple disciplines. The strong culture in local schools and colleges creates immediate common ground with exchange participants. It is one of the easiest entry points into the everyday life of the city.

University Life

Sant Gadge Baba Amravati University — named after the great Vidarbha saint and social reformer — serves as the city's intellectual and social hub, hosting seminars, cultural events, and AIESEC programmes that connect exchange participants with local students. The university's character reflects the region: earnest, collaborative, and rooted.

Street Food Trail

Amravati mornings begin with Pohe — rice flakes tempered with mustard, curry leaves, and chillies — followed by Saoji-style spiced dishes that define the city's culinary identity and are found almost nowhere else. The evening food scene around Rajapeth and the old city lanes is equally distinctive. Eating here is an act of regional discovery.

Village Immersion

Unlike most AIESEC exchanges, Amravati frequently offers participants direct exposure to surrounding villages and tribal communities — Korku, Gond, and Warli settlements within day-trip distance. These encounters, facilitated through local NGO partnerships, are among the most formative experiences available on any Indian exchange. It is the kind of exposure most programmes cannot offer and few participants expect.

Wildlife Sanctuary

Nagzira Wildlife Sanctuary in nearby Gondia is a compact but biodiverse reserve of mixed teak and bamboo forest sheltering tigers, leopards, gaur, and exceptional raptors. Small enough to explore meaningfully on a weekend trip, it remains almost entirely off the tourist circuit. That absence of crowds makes it all the more rewarding.

Practical Information

Everything you need to know before arriving in Amravati.

Accommodation

Options include hostels, host families, and shared flats — ideally within a 4 km radius of your workplace. Your local committee will help arrange everything.

Cost of Living

Expect to spend approximately ₹7,000–9,000 per month covering daily travel, meals, and miscellaneous expenses. Amravati is very affordable for exchange participants.

Transportation

Auto-rickshaws, shared taxis, and MSRTC buses are the primary modes of transport. Amravati is a compact city — easy to navigate on two wheels or by foot in many areas.

Visa

You'll need a valid Indian visa for your exchange. Your local committee will provide a detailed visa guide and assist with the application process.

Health & Safety

Amravati has solid healthcare infrastructure including government and private hospitals. Your local committee provides comprehensive safety guidelines and 24/7 support.

Language

Marathi is the primary language, with Hindi spoken widely across the city. English is well understood in academic and institutional settings.

SIM Card & Internet

Jio and Airtel offer strong 4G coverage across Amravati. Pick up a prepaid SIM at any mobile store with your passport — plans are affordable and fast.

Cultural Etiquette

Dress modestly at temples — cover your head at Ambadevi Temple and remove footwear at all religious entrances. "Kay Ho!" is a wonderful Marathi greeting that locals will love hearing from you.
