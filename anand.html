Discover Anand

VVN — Varanasi, Vindhyachal, Nanded — is one of AIESEC India's most unique Local Committees, anchored in some of India's most spiritually significant cities. Varanasi, the eternal city on the Ganges, has been continuously inhabited for over 3,000 years. Together, these cities offer exchange participants an unmatched plunge into India's sacred soul.

From dawn ghats rituals on the Ganges to the rooftop cafés of Assi Ghat, the Anand experience is unlike anything else in the world — a living, breathing encounter with ancient civilisation that will leave you permanently transformed.

City Highlights

What makes Anand unforgettable.

Ganga Aarti

Every evening at Dashashwamedh Ghat, seven priests perform the Ganga Aarti in choreographed unison — fire, flower, and incense offered to the river as hundreds of pilgrims watch from boats and the steps. The ceremony follows an ancient liturgical structure and lasts about 45 minutes. Nothing else in India quite prepares you for it.

The 84 Ghats

Each of Varanasi's 84 ghats has its own identity — some for ritual bathing, some for cremation, some for morning yoga and scholarly debate. Walking the full length at sunrise, as the city wakes and the river turns gold, is the essential Varanasi experience. It takes around two hours and is different every time.

Kashi Vishwanath

The Kashi Vishwanath temple is one of Hinduism's 12 Jyotirlingas and among its most sacred sites — rebuilt by Ahilyabai Holkar in 1780 and expanded with the Kashi Vishwanath Corridor in 2021. The corridor restored the river view and doubled the approach space for pilgrims. The atmosphere inside at dawn puja is unlike any other temple in India.

Sarnath

Ten kilometres from Varanasi, Sarnath is where the Buddha delivered his first sermon after enlightenment — the site where Buddhism began as a teaching tradition. The 5th-century Dhamek Stupa marks the spot; the Sarnath Museum holds the original Ashoka capital that became India's national emblem. It is one of Buddhism's four holiest sites.

Banarasi Cuisine

Varanasi's food culture is governed by ritual and time — kachori-sabzi eaten by pilgrims at dawn, thandai and bhang lassi through the day, and Banarasi paan to close the evening. The chaat at Kachori Gali and the sweets at Deena Chaat Bhandar have been drawing people for generations. Eating in Varanasi is inseparable from the experience of the city itself.

Banarasi Silk

The Banarasi silk weaving tradition is worked on handlooms by Muslim master weavers whose families have practised the craft for generations. The finest pieces incorporate real gold thread and can take months to complete; the silk markets of Vishwanath Gali remain the definitive source. The craft is UNESCO-recognised and entirely living.

BHU Campus

Banaras Hindu University, founded in 1916 on 1,350 acres, is one of Asia's largest residential universities — a campus city with its own temples, museums, and evening culture. The Vishwanath Temple at its centre and the Bharat Kala Bhavan museum with 11,000 artworks make it a destination in itself. Its academic energy and architectural grandeur are equally impressive.

Boat Rides on the Ganges

A wooden rowboat taken onto the Ganges before sunrise — as the ghats light up and smoke rises from the Manikarnika cremation fires — is one of the most profound experiences available in India. The river at this hour has a stillness that makes Varanasi's spiritual reputation entirely comprehensible. Most visitors count it among the defining moments of their time in India.

Practical Information

Everything you need to know before arriving in Anand.

Accommodation

Options include hostels, host families, and shared flats — ideally within a 4 km radius of your workplace. Your local committee will help arrange everything.

Cost of Living

Living costs in Anand are very affordable — expect ₹6,000–9,000/month for food, accommodation, and travel.

Transportation

Cycle rickshaws, auto-rickshaws, and shared jeeps navigate Varanasi's winding lanes. Ola/Uber are available for longer distances.

Visa

You'll need a valid Indian visa for your exchange. Your local committee will provide a detailed visa guide and assist with the application process.

Health & Safety

Good hospitals and clinics are available including BHU hospital. Your LC will provide a full health support guide on arrival.

Language

Hindi and Bhojpuri are spoken locally; English is understood in tourist areas and educational institutions.

SIM Card & Internet

Pick up a Jio or Airtel SIM on arrival. Coverage is reliable in city areas though remote temple zones may vary.

Cultural Etiquette

Anand has a warm and welcoming culture. Dress very modestly at all ghats and temples. Always remove shoes before entering sacred spaces.
