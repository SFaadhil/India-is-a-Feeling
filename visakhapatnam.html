Discover Visakhapatnam

Visakhapatnam — affectionately called Vizag — is Andhra Pradesh's largest city and a port city of extraordinary beauty. Flanked by the Eastern Ghats and the Bay of Bengal, Vizag offers stunning beaches, ancient Buddhist heritage sites, and a burgeoning industrial and IT corridor that has earned it the title "City of Destiny."

For exchange participants, Vizag delivers an authentic South Indian coastal experience — from morning chai on Rushikonda Beach to evenings at Kailasagiri Hill — in a city that is rapidly transforming into a world-class destination.

City Highlights

What makes Visakhapatnam unforgettable.

Rushikonda Beach

Rushikonda stretches in a long arc of golden sand on Vizag's northern coast, where the Eastern Ghats meet the Bay of Bengal at an angle that creates good surf and reliable wind. The beach is cleaner and less crowded than most urban Indian beaches, partly because it lacks the infrastructure that usually accompanies fame. Early mornings here, with the light coming off the water and the hills behind, are among the best reasons to be in Vizag.

Kailasagiri Hill

Kailasagiri is Vizag seen from above — a hilltop park where a monumental white Shiva and Parvati preside over a view that takes in the entire bay, the port, and the low hills of the city. The ropeway from the base is the most pleasant way up; the garden at the top is large enough to walk for an hour. The panorama at dusk, when the bay catches the last light, is the most complete view of the city available.

Submarine Museum

INS Kurusura served the Indian Navy for 31 years before being permanently moored on Vizag's Ramakrishna Beach as a museum. The interior — entirely accessible on a self-guided tour — shows exactly how the crew of 40 lived and operated in the compressed space of a working Soviet-era submarine. It is one of the most genuinely interesting military museums in India and takes about 45 minutes to explore.

Araku Valley

The Kirandul Express winds through 58 tunnels and 84 bridges to reach Araku — and every kilometre of the journey through Eastern Ghats tribal territory is the destination as much as the valley itself. Araku grows some of India's finest coffee; the tribal villages accessible from the valley are among the most intact in Andhra Pradesh. The journey should be taken one-way on the train and returned by road.

Andhra Cuisine

Andhra food makes no apologies — the gongura pachadi, the Vizag fish curry, and the rayalaseema-influenced meals here are among the spiciest and most flavour-forward cooking in India. The coastal availability of fresh fish — pomfret, prawns, surmai — adds a dimension unavailable inland. Eating in Vizag, particularly the seafood, is one of the principal pleasures of being here.

Buddhist Heritage

On the coastal hills north of the city, the ancient monasteries of Thotlakonda and Bavikonda overlook the Bay of Bengal from positions established by Buddhist monks in the 2nd century BCE. The rock-cut viharas, stupa foundations, and meditation cells have been partially excavated; the setting on windy hilltops above the sea is exceptional. Vizag's Buddhist heritage is among the most significant and least visited on any coastal circuit in India.

Simhachalam Temple

Perched on a hill above the city, the Simhachalam temple — dedicated to Lord Narasimha — is a masterwork of Kalinga-style stone carving and one of the 18 most important Narasimha shrines in Hinduism. The architecture, the position overlooking the bay, and the devotional intensity of its pilgrim community make it significantly more impressive than its relative obscurity outside Andhra suggests. The dawn viewing is the most atmospheric time to visit.

Port & IT Corridor

Vizag Port is one of India's busiest deep-water ports, handling over 70 million tonnes of cargo annually, while the VSEZ and the ITES corridor along the beach road are building a technology economy alongside the industrial one. The result is a city in genuine transition — from steel-port-industrial to services-and-knowledge, with both identities running simultaneously. For exchange participants, this moment in Vizag's development is worth being part of.

Practical Information

Everything you need to know before arriving in Visakhapatnam.

Accommodation

Options include hostels, host families, and shared flats — ideally within a 4 km radius of your workplace. Your local committee will help arrange everything.

Cost of Living

Vizag is very affordable — expect ₹7,000–9,000/month for food, travel, and daily expenses.

Transportation

City buses (APSRTC), auto-rickshaws, and Ola/Uber connect all areas. The city is also well-served by rail and air.

Visa

You'll need a valid Indian visa for your exchange. Your local committee will provide a detailed visa guide and assist with the application process.

Health & Safety

Strong healthcare infrastructure including King George Hospital and several private multispecialties. Your LC provides full health guidance.

Language

Telugu is the local language; English is widely spoken in business and educational settings.

SIM Card & Internet

Jio, Airtel, and BSNL all offer good coverage in Vizag. Pick up a SIM at the airport or any local outlet.

Cultural Etiquette

Andhra Pradesh has a deep tradition of hospitality. Remove shoes before entering temples; dress modestly at religious sites.
