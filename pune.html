Discover Pune

Pune is Maharashtra's cultural capital — a city where ancient Maratha history lives alongside modern campuses, thriving tech parks, and a coffee culture that never sleeps. Whether you're exploring Shaniwar Wada, trekking Sinhagad Fort, or diving into the buzzing Koregaon Park café scene, every corner of Pune pulses with energy.

For exchange participants, Pune offers the rare combination of a cosmopolitan, English-friendly environment and deep-rooted Indian traditions. The city's large student population makes it welcoming, dynamic, and endlessly enriching.

City Highlights

What makes Pune unforgettable.

Shaniwar Wada

The iconic Peshwa-era palace and its light-and-sound shows bring Maratha history alive every evening.

Sinhagad Fort

A trekker's paradise just outside the city — the fort offers breathtaking views and stories of legendary Maratha battles.

Koregaon Park

Pune's cosmopolitan heart — lined with boutiques, cafés, and the famous Osho International Meditation Resort.

Lavasa City

India's first planned hill city, a short drive from Pune, blends European architecture with lush Western Ghats scenery.

Puranpoli & Misal

Pune's culinary identity — from sweet puranpoli to fiery misal pav, the flavours are bold and unforgettable.

Ganesh Festival

Pune's 11-day Ganesh Chaturthi is legendary — the city transforms into a riot of processions, music, and devotion.

Aga Khan Palace

A historic palace where Mahatma Gandhi was held during the Quit India Movement — now a museum and memorial.

Tech & Startup Hub

Pune's Hinjewadi IT park and thriving startup ecosystem make it one of India's fastest-growing innovation centres.

Practical Information

Everything you need to know before arriving in Pune.

Accommodation

Options include hostels, host families, and shared flats — ideally within a 4 km radius of your workplace. Your local committee will help arrange everything.

Cost of Living

Expect to spend ₹9,000–12,000/month on food, travel, and daily expenses. The city is affordable by metro standards.

Transportation

Get around via PMPML city buses, Ola/Uber, and auto-rickshaws. Pune also has a growing metro network connecting key areas.

Visa

You'll need a valid Indian visa for your exchange. Your local committee will provide a detailed visa guide and assist with the application process.

Health & Safety

Pune has world-class hospitals including Ruby Hall Clinic and Jehangir Hospital. Your LC will brief you on emergency contacts.

Language

Marathi is the local language but English and Hindi are widely spoken across the city, especially in tech and educational areas.

SIM Card & Internet

Pick up a Jio, Airtel, or Vi SIM at the airport or local mobile store. 4G/5G coverage is excellent city-wide.

Cultural Etiquette

Dress respectfully at temples; festivals are a highlight — join the Ganesh Chaturthi celebrations! Greet with "Namaskar."
