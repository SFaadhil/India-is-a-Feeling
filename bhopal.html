Discover Bhopal

Bhopal, the capital of Madhya Pradesh, sits around two lakes at the heart of the country — Bhoj Tal (Upper Lake) and the smaller Lower Lake — giving the city its enduring nickname, the City of Lakes. Founded in its modern form by Dost Mohammad Khan in the early 18th century, Bhopal is equally known as the City of Begums, having been ruled for over a century by a remarkable line of female Muslim rulers who built its mosques, gardens, and palaces.

Beyond the old city's minarets and bazaars, Bhopal is a gateway to two UNESCO World Heritage Sites — the prehistoric rock art of Bhimbetka and the Buddhist stupas of Sanchi — making it one of India's richest short radii for history, alongside a modern capital's parks, campuses, and cultural institutions.

City Highlights

What makes Bhopal unforgettable.

Taj-ul-Masajid

One of Asia's largest mosques, the Taj-ul-Masajid ('Crown of Mosques') was commissioned by Shah Jahan Begum in the late 19th century and completed decades later in 1985. Its pink facade, three white domes, and twin minarets make it a defining image of Bhopal's skyline. The vast courtyard can hold tens of thousands of worshippers during Friday prayers and Ramzan.

Bhoj Tal (Upper Lake)

Built nearly a thousand years ago under a dam attributed to Raja Bhoj, the Upper Lake spans over 30 square kilometres and remains Bhopal's primary source of drinking water as well as its favourite evening spot. A Ramsar-recognised wetland, its promenade, boat rides, and sunset views over the water are the city's most unmistakable rhythm.

Gauhar Mahal & the Begums of Bhopal

Built in 1820 by Bhopal's first female ruler, Qudsia Begum, Gauhar Mahal blends Hindu and Mughal architectural styles along the lake's edge. It marks the start of a rare 107-year run of women rulers — Begums who modernised the city, built its institutions, and left Bhopal one of the only Indian cities shaped almost entirely by female statecraft.

Bhimbetka Rock Shelters

Roughly 45 kilometres south of Bhopal, the Bhimbetka rock shelters hold paintings dating back over 10,000 years, among the oldest evidence of human life in India. A UNESCO World Heritage Site set into sandstone cliffs and dense forest, the caves depict hunting scenes, dancing figures, and animals in pigments that have survived since the Mesolithic era.

Sanchi Stupa

An hour from Bhopal, the Great Stupa at Sanchi was commissioned by Emperor Ashoka in the 3rd century BCE and remains one of the oldest stone structures in India. Its intricately carved gateways narrate the life of the Buddha and rank among the finest examples of early Buddhist art. Along with Bhimbetka, it makes Bhopal one of India's best bases for a day trip into deep history.

Van Vihar National Park

Bordering the Upper Lake within the city limits, Van Vihar is a rare urban national park where tigers, leopards, and gharials live in naturalistic enclosures. Declared a national park in 1979, its lakeside walking trails make it as much a place for a quiet evening as a wildlife sanctuary — an unusual convenience for a state capital.

Moti Masjid & the Old City

Modelled on Delhi's Jama Masjid and completed in 1860 under Sikandar Begum, the Moti Masjid anchors Bhopal's old city, where narrow lanes open into the bustle of Chowk Bazaar and its food stalls. Wandering the old city at dusk, past bangle markets and centuries-old gateways, is the clearest window into Bhopal's Nawabi-era character.

Bharat Bhavan

Designed by architect Charles Correa and opened in 1982, Bharat Bhavan is a multi-arts centre built into a hillside overlooking the Upper Lake, housing a museum of tribal and folk art, a repertory theatre, and a poetry library. It represents the other half of Bhopal's identity — a modern capital investing heavily in culture alongside its historic core.

Practical Information

Everything you need to know before arriving in Bhopal.

Accommodation

Options include hostels, host families, and shared flats around New Market, Arera Colony, and the campus areas — ideally within a short commute of your workplace. Your local committee will help arrange everything.

Cost of Living

Bhopal is affordable for a state capital — expect ₹7,000–9,000/month for food, accommodation, and travel.

Transportation

City buses, auto-rickshaws, e-rickshaws, and Ola/Uber cover the city well. Bhopal is compact enough that most areas are a short ride from each other.

Visa

You'll need a valid Indian visa for your exchange. Your local committee will provide a detailed visa guide and assist with the application process.

Health & Safety

AIIMS Bhopal and Hamidia Hospital, along with numerous private clinics, provide reliable healthcare across the city. Your LC will provide emergency contacts and healthcare guidance.

Language

Hindi is the primary language, carrying a distinct Bhopali dialect shaped by Urdu and the city's Nawabi history; English is understood in professional and academic settings.

SIM Card & Internet

Pick up a Jio or Airtel SIM on arrival. 4G/5G coverage is reliable across the city, including around the lakes and old city.

Cultural Etiquette

Bhopal blends Hindu and Muslim traditions closely, so dress modestly at both mosques and temples, and expect a slower, more traditional pace than India's larger metros.
