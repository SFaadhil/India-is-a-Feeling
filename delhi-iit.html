Discover Delhi — IIT

Delhi is India's capital and one of the world's great megacities — a place where 5,000 years of civilisation coexist with cutting-edge modernity. AIESEC at IIT Delhi places exchange participants at the heart of this extraordinary intersection, on one of Asia's finest technology campuses.

From the research laboratories of IIT Delhi to the ancient Mughal monuments of Old Delhi — from the startup incubators of Connaught Place to the art galleries of Lodhi Colony — Delhi through the lens of AIESEC IIT offers an exchange experience of truly infinite possibilities.

City Highlights

What makes Delhi IIT unforgettable.

IIT Delhi Campus

One of India's premier technology institutes — a hub of world-class research, innovation, startup culture, and AIESEC's dynamic energy in the heart of the capital.

India Gate & Mughal Heritage

From the Red Fort and Jama Masjid to India Gate and Humayun's Tomb — Delhi's monuments are a living museum spanning 5,000 years of civilisation.

Delhi Metro

One of Asia's finest metro systems — modern, fast, air-conditioned, and connecting every corner of the capital with impressive efficiency and reliability.

Old Delhi & Chandni Chowk

The walled city of Old Delhi — Chandni Chowk's legendary markets, Jama Masjid's grandeur, and the finest Mughlai food you'll ever taste in your life.

Incredible Food Scene

From Paranthe Wali Gali and Karim's to Connaught Place's global restaurants — Delhi's food scene is epic, diverse, and endlessly rewarding.

Lodhi Art District

Delhi's thriving outdoor art gallery — Lodhi Colony's world-class murals and street art transform a residential neighbourhood into an open-air museum.

Entrepreneurship & Innovation

IITD's startup ecosystem, incubators, and distinguished alumni network create a vibrant environment for innovation, social impact, and entrepreneurial ambition.

Cultural Melting Pot

Delhi is a microcosm of all India — every state, cuisine, language, and cultural tradition coexists in this extraordinary, endlessly surprising city.

Practical Information

Everything you need to know before arriving in Delhi IIT.

Accommodation

Options include hostels, host families, and shared flats — ideally within a 4 km radius of your workplace. Your local committee will help arrange everything.

Cost of Living

Delhi is India's capital and living costs reflect its scale. Budget ₹12,000–15,000 per month for transport, meals, and daily expenses. Accommodation varies widely by location and type.

Transportation

Delhi Metro is the fastest and most reliable way to navigate the capital. Ola/Uber, DTC buses, and e-rickshaws complete last-mile connectivity throughout the city and campus.

Visa

You'll need a valid Indian visa for your exchange. Your local committee will provide a detailed visa guide and assist with the application process.

Health & Safety

Delhi has world-class healthcare facilities including AIIMS, Apollo, and Fortis. Your local committee provides comprehensive health guidelines and 24/7 emergency support.

Language

Hindi and English are both official languages of Delhi. The city is a remarkable linguistic mosaic — you'll hear Punjabi, Urdu, Bengali, and dozens of regional languages.

SIM Card & Internet

Jio, Airtel, and Vi offer excellent 4G/5G coverage across Delhi and the IIT campus. Pick up a prepaid SIM at the airport or any mobile store with your passport.

Cultural Etiquette

Dress modestly at mosques, temples, and heritage sites. Remove shoes at religious entrances. Delhi is cosmopolitan but fast-paced — be assertive, curious, and open to its extraordinary energy.
