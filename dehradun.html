Discover Dehradun

Dehradun is India's school city — cradled in the magnificent Doon Valley between the Ganga and Yamuna rivers, with the snow-capped Himalayas visible on clear winter mornings. This is a city where intellect and nature coexist in extraordinary harmony, and where India's finest institutions have chosen to build their homes.

AIESEC in Dehradun offers exchange participants a rare combination of academic rigour and natural beauty. From IIT Roorkee to AIIMS Rishikesh, from the Forest Research Institute to the Tibetan community of Clement Town, Dehradun is a city that consistently produces the feeling of winning — one beautiful experience at a time.

City Highlights

What makes Dehradun unforgettable.

Doon Valley

Dehradun sits in a basin where the Ganga and Yamuna river systems divide — a natural amphitheatre enclosed by the Shivalik range and the Greater Himalayas. The resulting microclimate — mild winters, heavy monsoon, never the extremes of the plains — gives the city a particular quality of light and air. It is one of the most physically beautiful mid-sized cities in India.

Premier Institutions

Within 50 kilometres of Dehradun sit IIT Roorkee, AIIMS Rishikesh, the Wildlife Institute of India, and the Forest Research Institute — making this cluster one of the most concentrated zones of research excellence in India outside the major metros. The Doon School and Rashtriya Indian Military College add further institutional weight. The academic density gives the city a specific, serious intellectual character.

Robber's Cave

Robber's Cave — Guchhupani to locals — is a 600-metre limestone gorge where a seasonal stream flows directly through the rock, creating natural pools accessible only by wading through shin-deep water. The site is 8 kilometres from the city centre and takes about two hours to walk fully. It is genuinely distinctive and entirely unlike any other urban day trip in North India.

Gateway to Mussoorie

The road from Dehradun to Mussoorie climbs 1,500 metres in 30 kilometres, and the view from the top across the Doon Valley and toward the high Himalayas is among the finest short road trips in Uttarakhand. Mussoorie's Mall Road, Landour's quiet lanes, and the Company Garden are all within a half-day. The drive down at sunset is especially good.

Tibetan Culture

The Clement Town area houses one of India's largest Tibetan communities — monks, artisans, and long-established families who rebuilt their institutions in exile from the 1960s onwards. The Mindrolling Monastery, with its 60-metre stupa, is the most architecturally impressive Tibetan Buddhist site in India. Wandering through the Tibetan market and eating momos from roadside stalls here is a genuine cultural immersion.

Forest Research Institute

The Forest Research Institute is one of the most architecturally magnificent buildings in India — a 450-acre Greco-Roman campus built by the British in 1906 that houses six natural history museums covering botany, forestry, timber, and ecology. The building itself, and the avenues of trees that surround it, are worth an afternoon even before the museums. It is among India's most under-visited great institutions.

Rishikesh & Haridwar

Haridwar, 55 kilometres from Dehradun, is one of Hinduism's seven sacred cities — where the Ganga emerges onto the plains and the evening Ganga Aarti at Har ki Pauri draws tens of thousands daily. Rishikesh, 45 kilometres away, has built a parallel identity as the global capital of yoga. Both are easy day trips and together represent two of the most spiritually charged environments in India.

Pahadi Food & Markets

Dehradun's Paltan Bazaar and old city markets deal in produce unavailable in the plains: Himalayan red rice, mandua flour, pahadi cucumber, and wild-harvested herbs. The local cuisine — aloo ke gutke, kafuli, chainsoo — is modest, deeply regional, and worth seeking out beyond the dhaba standard. The Tibetan food in Clement Town adds an entirely different register.

Practical Information

Everything you need to know before arriving in Dehradun.

Accommodation

Options include hostels, host families, and shared flats — ideally within a 4 km radius of your workplace. Your local committee will help arrange everything.

Cost of Living

Dehradun is pleasantly affordable. Expect ₹8,000–10,000 per month for daily transport, meals, and living expenses. Accommodation costs vary but options are plentiful.

Transportation

Auto-rickshaws, Ola/Uber taxis, and Uttarakhand Roadways (UKTC) buses serve the city well. Dehradun is compact and comfortable to navigate — many participants enjoy cycling locally.

Visa

You'll need a valid Indian visa for your exchange. Your local committee will provide a detailed visa guide and assist with the application process.

Health & Safety

Dehradun has solid healthcare including AIIMS Rishikesh nearby and multiple quality private hospitals in the city. Your local committee provides safety guidance and round-the-clock support.

Language

Hindi and Pahari are the local languages. English is well understood at institutions and by younger residents. The people of Uttarakhand are famously warm and gracious to visitors.

SIM Card & Internet

Jio and Airtel provide strong 4G coverage across Dehradun. Pick up a prepaid SIM at Jolly Grant Airport or any mobile store with your passport — affordable and reliable.

Cultural Etiquette

Uttarakhand is deeply reverent — dress modestly at temples and monasteries, remove shoes before entering, and observe silence at sacred spaces. Locals appreciate a respectful "Namaste" and genuine curiosity about their culture.
